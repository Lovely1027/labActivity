Let's study N2 level!

What is N2 Level?

N2 is the second-highest level of the Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT), just below N1. At the N2 level, learners are expected to have advanced-intermediate proficiency in Japanese. They should be able to understand a wide range of topics and handle complex language used in formal and informal contexts. The N2 level signifies that you are well-equipped to live and work in Japan, engage in deeper conversations, and understand more sophisticated texts.

Key Characteristics of the N2 Level:

Vocabulary:

At N2, learners should know around 6,000 words. The vocabulary covers more advanced concepts and includes words that are commonly found in news reports, formal settings, literature, and academic discussions.

Examples of N2 vocabulary include:

  1. 意欲 (いよく, iyoku) — "desire," "willingness"
  2. 成熟 (せいじゅく, seijuku) — "maturity"
  3. 従う (したがう, shitagau) — "to follow" or "to comply"
  4. 景気 (けいき, keiki) — "economic condition"
  5. 進捗 (しんちょく, shinchoku) — "progress" (e.g., work progress)

Grammar:

N2 grammar involves complex sentence structures and the use of nuanced expressions in both formal and informal contexts. Learners will need to understand subtle differences in meaning and use grammar structures to express more sophisticated ideas.

Examples of N2 grammar points include:

  1. ～といっても (to ittemo) — "although it is said that" (used to express contrast or contradiction)
    簡単だと言っても、やってみると難しい (かんたんだといっても、やってみるとむずかしい, Kantan da to ittemo, yatte miru to muzukashii)
    — "Although it’s said to be easy, it’s difficult when you actually try it."

  2. ～を問わず (wotoiwazu) — "regardless of"
    年齢を問わず、誰でも参加できます (ねんれいをとわず、だれでもさんかできます, Nenrei o towazu, daredemo sanka dekimasu)
    — "Anyone can participate, regardless of age."

  3. ～にかかわらず (ni kakawarazu) — "regardless of"
    天気にかかわらず、予定通り行います (てんきにかかわらず、よていどおりおこないます, Tenki ni kakawarazu, yotei dōri okonaimasu)
    — "We will go ahead with the plan regardless of the weather."

  4. ～というものではない (to iu mono de wa nai) — "does not mean" or "is not necessarily"
    努力すれば成功するというものではない (どりょくすればせいこうするというものではない, Doryoku sureba seikou suru to iu mono de wa nai)
    — "It doesn’t necessarily mean that you will succeed just by making an effort."

Kanji:

  • N2 learners are expected to know about 1,000 Kanji. These Kanji include both frequently used characters in formal writing (e.g., newspapers, books) and those used in more specialized fields (e.g., literature, business, science).
  • N2 Kanji may appear in complex compounds or less common readings.

Examples of N2-level Kanji include:

  1. 議 (ぎ, gi) — "debate" or "discussion"
  2. 環 (かん, kan) — "circle" or "environment"
  3. 証 (しょう, shō) — "proof" or "evidence"
  4. 罪 (つみ, tsumi) — "crime" or "guilt"
  5. 報告 (ほうこく, hōkoku) — "report" or "announcement"

Listening:

  • Listening comprehension at N2 requires the ability to understand fast, natural speech in a variety of contexts. This includes news reports, formal presentations, conversations about complex topics, and discussions on specialized subjects like economics, science, and culture.
  • N2 learners should be able to follow extended conversations and catch the main ideas, even if they are surrounded by complex vocabulary or abstract ideas.
  • Example scenario: Understanding a radio broadcast discussing current events or a formal business meeting about market trends.

Reading:

  • N2 learners can read and understand texts on a wide range of topics, including newspapers, magazines, reports, and novels. They are expected to be able to handle abstract or complex topics and analyze the meaning of various passages, including the subtle implications behind the language used.
  • Example reading material: A newspaper article discussing social issues or a research paper on a technological development.

Writing:

  • N2 learners can write detailed essays and formal letters, and are able to express opinions and discuss topics in a clear and organized manner. Their writing reflects an understanding of appropriate style, tone, and register for various situations.
  • Example: Writing a letter of complaint, a formal request, or an opinion essay on a societal issue.

JLPT N2 Test Structure:

The JLPT N2 test consists of three main sections:

Language Knowledge (Vocabulary and Grammar):

  • This section evaluates your understanding of intermediate to advanced vocabulary and grammar.
  • You will be tested on contextual usage and appropriate grammar in sentences. It requires knowledge of both spoken and written forms.

Reading:

  • This section tests your ability to understand written Japanese and includes a range of materials, such as short passages, newspaper articles, and longer texts.
  • You’ll be asked to identify the main ideas, understand details, and interpret nuances in the texts.

Listening:

  • This section tests your ability to understand spoken Japanese in various real-life situations. It includes conversations, announcements, interviews, and dialogues that may involve complex expressions.
  • You’ll need to grasp the main points and specific details of the conversation.

Example Sentences for N2 Learners:

  1. その提案は非常に興味深いと思います (そのていあんはひじょうにきょうみぶかいとおもいます, Sono teian wa hijō ni kyōmi-bukai to omoimasu)
    — "I think that proposal is very interesting."

  2. 最近、環境問題が重要なテーマになっています (さいきん、かんきょうもんだいがじゅうようなテーマになっています, Saikin, kankyō mondai ga jūyō na tēma ni natteimasu)
    — "Recently, environmental issues have become an important theme."

  3. この映画はストーリーよりも映像の美しさが際立っています (このえいがはストーリーよりもえいぞうのうつくしさがきわだっています, Kono eiga wa sutōrī yori mo eizō no utsukushisa ga kiwadatteimasu)
    — "This movie stands out more for its beautiful visuals than for its story."

  4. 彼の説明は明確で、納得できます (かれのせつめいはめいかくで、なっとくできます, Kare no setsumei wa meikaku de, nattoku dekimasu)
    — "His explanation is clear and convincing."

Study Tips for N2:

  1. Master N3 content first: Make sure you’re comfortable with N3 before tackling N2 material, as N2 builds on the foundation laid by N3.

  2. Textbooks: Use resources like "Sou Matome N2" or "Nihongo So-Matome" series, which are specifically designed to cover the grammar, vocabulary, and Kanji at N2 level.

  3. Kanji Practice: Continue studying Kanji and vocabulary. Flashcards (like Anki or WaniKani) are very helpful for memorization.

  4. Practice with native material: Watch news shows, movies, dramas, or podcasts in Japanese. Reading novels, newspapers, and magazines will also help.

  5. Mock Exams: Take mock JLPT exams to familiarize yourself with the format and build your test-taking skills.

  6. Writing and Speaking: Practice writing essays, emails, and letters. Find a language partner to practice speaking about various topics, or use language exchange apps.

At the N2 level, you can engage in complex conversations, read challenging texts, and write detailed pieces with confidence. You should be able to handle work-related tasks, participate in formal discussions.
